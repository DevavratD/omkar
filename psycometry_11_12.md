# 🧠 SCORING RULES YOU SHOULD USE

## 1) Likert Scoring

* Strongly Disagree = 1
* Disagree = 2
* Not Sure = 3
* Agree = 4
* Strongly Agree = 5

## 2) Reverse Scoring

For `"direction": "reverse"`:

```text
new_score = 6 - response
```

So:

* 1 → 5
* 2 → 4
* 3 → 3
* 4 → 2
* 5 → 1

## 3) Construct Score

```text
construct_raw = sum(all items for that construct)
```

## 4) Normalize to 100

For 4-item constructs:

* min = 4
* max = 20

```text
normalized = ((raw - 4) / 16) * 100
```

For 3-item constructs:

* min = 3
* max = 15

```text
normalized = ((raw - 3) / 12) * 100
```

# ⚠️ What’s still true:

## 1) This is a **career-orientation psychometric**, not a validated published psychometric instrument

So don’t market it like:

> “scientifically proven personality diagnosis”

That would be dishonest.

What you *can* honestly say is:

> “A structured career orientation assessment based on work-style preferences, interests, and aptitude patterns.”

That’s solid. That’s defensible.

---

## 2) Some constructs overlap — and that’s normal

For example:

* `leadership_initiative`
* `enterprising`
* `social_orientation`

These are related but still useful separately **if your report wording is disciplined**.

If your report says:

> “You are a born CEO and natural leader and guaranteed entrepreneur”

then your system becomes clown-tier instantly.

---

## 3) The report quality matters more than the question count

A mediocre report can make a decent test feel fake.

A clean report can make a decent test feel professional.

So don’t obsess only over “52 questions”.
Obsess over:

* interpretation logic
* wording
* explanation quality
* consistency

That’s where the product becomes believable.
